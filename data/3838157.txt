In television programming an interstitial television show or wraparound program or wraparound segment refers to a short program that is often shown between movies or other events e g cast interviews after movies on premium channels The term can also refer to a narrative bridge between segments within a program such as the live action introductions to the animated segments in the Disney films Fantasia and Fantasia or the Simpson family s interludes during their annual Treehouse of Horror episodes Sometimes if a program finishes earlier than expected a short extra program may be inserted in
